# Personal Brand & Website Design — Martin Mana

**Date:** 2026-03-11
**Status:** Approved

## Core Identity

Martin Mana is an AI product strategist who also builds. Not a developer for hire — a diagnostician who identifies what needs to exist, designs the solution, and ships it end-to-end.

**Positioning statement:** You bring the problem. I find the solution and build it.

**Differentiator:** 10+ years of design background (art direction, graphic design, branding, packaging) that most engineers don't have. This isn't the offer — it's the silent advantage that makes everything he builds better.

**Career arc:** Art Direction → Graphic Design → Digital Marketing (2015) → Frontend Engineering (2019) → AI Product Development (now)

## Brand Attributes

- **Builder by nature** — 90% of 55+ projects are self-initiated ideas, not client briefs
- **Solo operator** — one-person product team, with collaborators when needed
- **Problem-first thinker** — doesn't build what he's told, builds what he's identified as the right solution
- **Stack-agnostic** — picks the right tool for the job (React, Svelte, Python, Swift, Electron, AI APIs)
- **Global** — works remotely, location-irrelevant

## The Process (Core Offering)

One integrated process, not separate services:

1. **Analyse** — deep-dive into the product, process, or business challenge
2. **Strategize** — identify what needs to exist (or change) and why
3. **Design & Build** — ship the solution end-to-end, AI-powered where it makes sense
4. **Teach** — transfer knowledge so the team understands what was built and why

Consulting and building are the same engagement. The teaching/talking extends this — sharing how he thinks about problems.

## Success Criteria (6-month priority order)

1. Inbound leads from people who want AI products built
2. Recognized as a thought leader in AI product development
3. Own products (Braintube, etc.) gain users through the site
4. Attractive to employers who value hybrid design+engineering+AI skills

## Site Structure

### 1. Hero
Sharp positioning statement + primary CTA. Communicates "AI product strategist who builds" in under 5 seconds. No service lists. One clear message.

### 2. The Process — "How I Work"
The Analyse → Strategize → Design & Build → Teach flow. This IS the pitch. Shows that clients bring problems, not specs.

### 3. Featured Work (3-4 case studies)
Deep showcases of AI products. Each one follows the process: what was the problem → what did I identify as the solution → what did I build → what was the outcome. Candidates: Braintube, RAGify, AI Chat Component, STIHL NZ performance work.

### 4. The Archive (200+ works)
Scrollable social proof of range and volume. Two lanes:
- **Products & Apps** — self-initiated builds (Braintube, Frello, budget app, music tools, etc.)
- **Design Legacy** — branding, packaging, logos, UI, exhibition design

Not the focus, but undeniable proof of depth. The "holy shit, he's done all this" moment.

### 5. Thought Leadership
Writing, talks, analysis content. Signals the teaching/communicator identity. Can launch with minimal content — the section itself declares intent.

### 6. Own Products
Links to live products with brief descriptions. Supports builder identity and drives product users.

### 7. About
Brief personal story. The career arc from art direction to AI product development. The narrative is the differentiator — this isn't someone who learned to code, it's someone whose entire career has been building toward this convergence.

### 8. Contact / CTA
"Let's talk" — low friction. Not a form with 15 fields. Conversation-first, matching the consulting positioning.

## Brand Voice

- Direct, confident, slightly irreverent
- Technical credibility without jargon walls
- Talks like a human, not a corporate site
- The "nerdy stuff, but cool" energy, sharpened
- Shows thinking process, not just outputs

## Design Direction

- Visually polished — he's a designer, the site must reflect that
- Structure shifts from "portfolio gallery" to "conversion-focused narrative"
- The design work in the archive section can showcase visual ability without it dominating the message

## What This Is NOT

- Not a service menu (no "AI Solutions, UX/UI, Web Dev, App Dev, Graphic Design" list)
- Not a portfolio gallery with 248 undifferentiated entries
- Not a "jack of all trades" pitch
- Not a hired-hand positioning — this is a strategist who builds

## Target Audience

- Startups needing an AI product built from zero
- Agencies needing a senior dev with strategic/design thinking
- Mid-size companies wanting to integrate AI into existing products
- Geographic: Global, remote-first
